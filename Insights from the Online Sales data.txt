Insights from the Online Sales dataset

1. Testing the first hypothesis: "Does a higher discount lead to a higher quantity ordered?"

The answer: No. It was proved by the Spearman Correlation test, and it showed a negative correlation value with p < 0.05. 

2. Testing the second hypothesis: "Do Online orders generate higher Revenue than In-store orders?"

The answer: No. It was proved by the Mann-Whitney U test, and it returned p > 0.05. So, it meant the result was not statistically significant. It can be seen easily from their median revenue prices, which are almost equal.

3. Testing the third hypothesis: "Does Revenue differ significantly across product Categories?" 

The answer: No. It was proved by the Kruskal-Wallis, where the p-value was higher than 0.05. So, H0 is incorrect. From the pairwise Mann-Whitney combination of values, it can be seen that all categories generated almost the same value.

4. Testing the fourth hypothesis: "Is revenue spread (variance) wider for High priority orders than Medium or Low?"

The answer: No. It was proved by Levene's test, where the p-value was higher than 0.05. Because the IQR (Interquartile Range) was almost the same range for both Low and Medium.

5. Testing the fifth hypothesis: "Is return rate associated with payment method?"

The answer: No. First of all, it was tested by the Chi-Square test, and the p-value was higher than 0.05. Secondly, the table also showed approximately close numbers.
