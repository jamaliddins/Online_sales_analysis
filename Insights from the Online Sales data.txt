Key Insights
1. Discount vs Quantity Ordered

No significant positive relationship was found between discount levels and quantity ordered.
Spearman correlation analysis showed a statistically significant negative relationship (p < 0.05), suggesting that higher discounts do not necessarily drive higher sales volume.

2. Sales Channel Performance (Online vs In-store)

Revenue does not significantly differ between online and in-store orders.
The Mann-Whitney U test (p > 0.05) indicates similar revenue distributions across both channels.

3. Revenue by Product Category

No statistically significant difference in revenue was found across product categories.
Kruskal-Wallis test results (p > 0.05) suggest relatively uniform revenue generation across categories.

4. Revenue Variability by Order Priority

Revenue variability is consistent across different order priority levels.
Levene’s test (p > 0.05) indicates no significant difference in variance between High, Medium, and Low priority orders.

5. Returns and Payment Methods

Return rates are not significantly associated with payment methods.
Chi-square test results (p > 0.05) suggest independence between payment type and return behavior.
